About this project

There is no imaging model behind NeuroScan — and that's the point.

This page explains exactly what the demo does, what it doesn't do, and why it's built this way.

What it is

A front-end concept exploring the interface and information design of an AI-assisted scan review tool: intake, upload, a staged progress sequence, a results view and a printable report.

What it isn't

It is not a medical device, not validated on any dataset, and not connected to any trained model. The "AI Analysis" screen is a scripted animation, not a running computation over your image.

How results are made

Result and report figures are derived from a simple hash of the uploaded file's name and size — enough to produce stable, repeatable numbers for demo purposes, nothing more.

Design intent

Why build a fake diagnostic tool at all?

Clinical software has a distinct visual language — dense data, careful hierarchy, restrained color used only where it means something. Recreating that language, honestly labelled as a demo, is a useful design exercise without any of the real-world risk of an unvalidated tool making health claims.

Every screen discloses itself

A banner runs across the top of every page in this project. The result and report screens repeat the disclosure in their own words, right next to the numbers they're describing.

Data stays local

Patient details and uploaded images are stored in your browser's local storage only. Nothing is transmitted to a server, logged, or shared — clearing your browsing data clears the demo entirely.

Have a real concern about a scan or symptom?

Please speak with a licensed physician or radiologist. No website — this one included — should be used to interpret medical imaging or delay a real evaluation.
